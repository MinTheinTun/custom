REHABILITATION & RECOVERY

GCC Centre provides inpatient therapy following a qualified hospital stay

STRENGTH, RENEWED

When you need the right plan for your body to heal and be at its best again, we provide both long-term care and short-term skilled nursing to help you recuperate, gain strength and get back on your feet as quickly as possible.

Ordinarily, rehabilitation can take a few days to weeks. Our skilled therapists will work with your primary care physician to design a treatment for your specific needs.

GCC Centre provides inpatient therapy, following a qualified hospital stay, to access our Olathe rehabilitation services. Our clinical specialties include, but aren’t limited to:

  • Occupational and physical therapy for seniors
  • Speech therapy for seniors
  • Vital Stim and E-Stim
  • Ultrasound
  • Heat and cold modalities
  • Neurologic and orthopedic rehabilitationl
  • Post-surgical reconditioning
  • Care management and discharge planning

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does rehabilitation include?

    Health Services at GCC Centre provides short- and long- term rehabilitation through physical, speech and occupational therapies. Resident and nonresident seniors can receive expert care from our skilled pathologists and therapists using the most advanced treatments and technologies. Senior rehabilitation treatment may include:

    • - Cardiac care
    • - Joint replacement
    • - Orthopedics
    • - Pain management
    • - Neurologic recovery
    • - Stroke recovery
    • - Wound healing
    • - Respiratory care
    • - Other major illness, event or surgery
  • What's the difference between occupational and physical therapies?

    Both help restore strength and mobility; however, occupational therapy helps people adapt to their social and physical environment through education in things like dressing, memory training and coordination exercises.

  • What can I expect from speech therapy?

    Our skilled speech and language pathologists address communication issues and swallowing dysfunction. They’ll design treatment plans to help with language ability, provide alternate communication strategies and give appropriate diet recommendations.
